Strategies for affordable housing in small towns


There is no one-size-fits-all solution to the affordable housing crisis, but there are some strategies that can be particularly effective in small towns.

One strategy is to develop community land trusts. These trusts purchase land and then make it available for affordable housing developments. The land is permanently protected from being sold at market value, ensuring that it remains affordable in perpetuity.

Another strategy is to work with existing homeowners to make their properties more affordable. This can be done through voluntary programs like housing co-ops, in which homeowners pool their resources to make housing more affordable for everyone. It can also be done through government programs that provide incentives for homeowners to make their properties available for low- and moderate-income residents.

These are just a few of the strategies that can be used to address the affordable housing crisis in small towns. For more information, please contact your local housing authority.
