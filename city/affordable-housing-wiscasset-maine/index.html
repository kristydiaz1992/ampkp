Wiscasset Housing Authority partners with local organizations to provide affordable housing


The Wiscasset Housing Authority (WHA) is committed to providing quality affordable housing to low- and moderate-income families in the community. In partnership with local organizations, the WHA is able to provide a variety of housing options to meet the needs of families.

The WHA partners with The Salvation Army to provide transitional housing for families who are working towards self-sufficiency. The Salvation Army provides case management and support services to help families achieve their goals.

The WHA also partners with Habitat for Humanity to provide homeownership opportunities for low- and moderate-income families. Habitat for Humanity builds and sells homes at an affordable price, with no interest charged on the mortgage. Families who partner with Habitat for Humanity are required to put in “sweat equity” hours to help build their own home or another Habitat home.

In addition to partnering with local organizations, the WHA also manages a variety of affordable housing developments. Woodland Heights is an affordable housing development that features one-, two-, and three-bedroom units. Woodland Heights is conveniently located near schools, shopping, and public transportation.
