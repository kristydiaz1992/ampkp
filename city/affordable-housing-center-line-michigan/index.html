The Benefits of Affordable Housing


Affordable housing is a term used for rental housing that is considered affordable to those with a lower income. In the United States, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) sets fair market rents (FMRs) for specific metropolitan areas, which is the 40th percentile of gross rents for typical, non-subsidized units in that area.

There are many benefits of affordable housing, both for those who live in it and for society as a whole.

For tenants, affordable housing can provide a stable and safe place to live, which can be a stepping stone to a better life. In addition, because affordable housing is often located in areas with good schools and other amenities, it can provide opportunities for tenants to improve their circumstances.

For society, affordable housing can help to reduce crime and improve mental and physical health. In addition, because affordable housing developments often create jobs during construction and provide economic opportunities for those who live in them, they can help to revitalize communities.

In sum, affordable housing is a critical part of ensuring that everyone has a chance to thrive.
