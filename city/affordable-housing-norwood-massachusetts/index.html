the history of affordable housing


The term “affordable housing” is used to describe dwelling units that are affordable by households with a median income or lower. In the United States, the concept of affordable housing has its roots in the New Deal era of the 1930s. The first affordable housing program was the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), which was established as part of the National Housing Act of 1934. The FHA’s mission was to promote home ownership by insuring mortgages and providing loans for home improvement.

The Second World War led to a housing crisis as millions of Americans left their homes to serve in the military or to work in defense plants. The FHA responded by insuring loans for the construction of war housing. After the war, the FHA continued to support the construction of affordable housing for families returning from service and for the growing number of families moving to the suburbs.

In the 1960s, the federal government began to expand its role in promoting affordable housing. The 1970s saw a number of changes to the federal government’s affordable housing programs. The Housing and Community Development Act of 1974 established the Community Development Block Grant program, which provided funding to local governments for the development of affordable housing. In addition, the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit was created to encourage the construction of rental housing for low-income households.

The 1980s and 1990s saw a number of changes to the federal government’s affordable housing programs.
