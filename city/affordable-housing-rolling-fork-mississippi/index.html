The Challenge of Creating More Affordable Housing


There is no question that affordable housing is a pressing need in communities across the United States. The National Low Income Housing Coalition reports that there is a shortage of 7.2 million affordable and available rental homes for extremely low income households, and the problem is only getting worse as rent prices continue to rise.

Creating more affordable housing is a challenge for policymakers and developers alike. One of the biggest obstacles is the high cost of land and construction. In order to make housing more affordable, it is necessary to bring down these costs.

One way to do this is to create incentives for developers to build more affordable housing. Inclusionary zoning ordinances, for example, require a certain percentage of units in new developments to be set aside for low- or moderate-income households. While these policies can be effective, they can also be controversial, as some argue that they make it more difficult for developers to turn a profit and thus discourage investment in affordable housing.

Another way to bring down the cost of affordable housing is to increase funding for affordable housing initiatives. This can be done at the local, state, and federal level, and can take the form of direct funding, tax breaks, or other types of financial assistance.

No matter what approach is taken, creating more affordable housing will require a concerted effort from all stakeholders. Only by working together can we hope to address this critical need.
