This act created the Federal Housing Administration, which provided insurance for loans used to build housing developments for low- and moderate-income families.

The Housing and Community Development Act of 1974 created the Section 8 program, which provided subsidies for rent payments for low-income families. This program is still in place today and helps millions of families afford a place to live.
