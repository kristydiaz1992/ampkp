10 Affordable Housing Developments in Rancho Palos Verdes


There is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how to make housing more affordable, as the needs of each community are unique. However, there are some general principles that can be applied to create more affordable housing developments.

1. Inclusionary zoning

Inclusionary zoning is a planning tool that requires a certain percentage of units in a new development to be set aside for low- or moderate-income households. This can help to create a mix of incomes within a development, making it more affordable for everyone.

2. Density bonuses

Density bonuses allow for increased density in exchange for the inclusion of affordable units. This can help to make a development more economically viable while still providing some affordable housing.

3. Community land trusts

Community land trusts are a way to keep land in the hands of the community, rather than private developers. This can help to ensure that new developments are created with the community’s needs in mind, rather than simply for profit.

4. Reducing parking requirements

Parking requirements can add a significant amount to the cost of a new development. Reducing or eliminating these requirements can help to make a development more affordable.

5. Micro-units

Micro-units are smaller than traditional units, and can be a more affordable option for those who don’t need a lot of space.

6. Co-housing

Co-housing developments are designed to promote a sense of community and encourage interaction between residents. This can help to reduce the overall cost of living, as residents share resources and support each other.

7. Modular construction

Modular construction is a way of building units in a factory setting, which can help to reduce the cost of construction.

8. Passive house design

Passive house design is a way of building homes that uses very little energy to heat and cool. This can help to reduce the overall cost of living, as energy bills are typically much lower.

9. Zero energy homes

Zero energy homes are designed to produce as much energy as they consume, through the use of solar panels and other renewable energy sources. This can help to make a home much more affordable to live in, as energy costs are effectively eliminated.

10. Accessory dwelling units

Accessory dwelling units (ADUs) are small homes that are built on the same property as a larger home. They can be a more affordable option for those who want to live in a certain area but can’t afford the cost of a traditional home.
