The history of affordable housing and its impact today


The term “affordable housing” first came into use in the United States in the 1930s, during the Great Depression. At that time, the federal government began providing financial assistance to state and local governments to help them build housing for low- and moderate-income families. In the 1940s, the federal government began offering tax incentives to encourage private developers to build affordable housing.

The impact of affordable housing today is far reaching. According to a report by the National Low Income Housing Coalition, there are 7.1 million extremely low income households in the United States, and only 4.2 million units of affordable and available housing. This leaves 3.9 million extremely low income households without a place to call home.

The lack of affordable housing has a ripple effect that goes beyond those who are directly affected. When families are forced to spend a large portion of their income on housing, they have less money to spend on other necessities, such as food, clothing, and healthcare. This can lead to poor health outcomes and increased rates of food insecurity and poverty.

The issue of affordable housing is one that must be addressed at all levels of government.
