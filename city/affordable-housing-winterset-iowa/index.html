The history of affordable housing


The history of affordable housing is a long and complicated one. In the United States, the idea of affordable housing didn’t really take off until the 1930s when the Great Depression hit and millions of people were left homeless. The government responded by creating programs like the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) and the Home Owners’ Loan Corporation (HOLC) which helped people get mortgages and buy homes.

After World War II, the government continued to invest in affordable housing, but the focus shifted from ownership to rental assistance. In the 1970s, the Section 8 program was created which provides voucher-based assistance to low-income renters. Today, there are a variety of affordable housing programs available, but the need for affordable housing still far exceeds the supply. The lack of affordable housing is a major problem in many cities across the country and it’s something that needs to be addressed urgently.
