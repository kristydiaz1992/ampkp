The Different Types of Affordable Housing in Woodland


There are several types of affordable housing in Woodland, each with their own unique benefits. Below, we will explore some of the most popular affordable housing options in the city.

1. Low-Income Housing: Low-income housing is designed for households that earn less than a certain amount each year. These units are typically subsidized by the government, which helps to keep rent costs low.

2. Senior Housing: Senior housing is designed for residents that are 55 years of age or older. These units often have special amenities and services that cater to the needs of seniors.

3. Section 8 Housing: Section 8 housing is a federally-funded program that provides housing assistance to low-income households. households that participate in the program pay a portion of their rent, with the government covering the rest.

4. Public Housing: Public housing is owned and operated by the government. These units are typically reserved for low-income households.

5. Housing Choice Vouchers: Housing choice vouchers are a form of government assistance that can be used to help pay rent for privately-owned units. households that participate in the program typically pay 30% of their income towards rent, with the government covering the rest.

6. Affordable Housing Developments: Affordable housing developments are privately-owned developments that have been designated as such by the government. These units must meet certain affordability requirements, such as having a certain percentage of units set aside for low-income households.

7. Community Land Trusts: Community land trusts are nonprofit organizations that work to create and preserve affordable housing. These units are typically owned by the trust, with residents leasing the land from the trust.
