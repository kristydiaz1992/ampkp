creative solutions for affordable housing in small towns


One of the most pressing issues facing small towns is the lack of affordable housing. This is a problem that affects everyone, from young families trying to find a place to live to seniors on fixed incomes who are struggling to keep up with rising property taxes.

There are a number of creative solutions that can help to address the issue of affordable housing in small towns. One option is to work with local developers to create mixed-use developments that include both residential and commercial space. This can help to increase the overall supply of housing in a town while also providing opportunities for new businesses to move in and create jobs.

Another solution is to create accessory dwelling units (ADUs) such as mother-in-law apartments or granny flats. These units can provide additional housing options for families or individuals while also generating income for the property owner.

Finally, towns can also encourage the development of co-housing communities. These communities are designed to promote a sense of community and connection among residents while also providing affordable housing options.

By working together to find creative solutions, small towns can begin to address the issue of affordable housing and make their communities more livable for everyone.
