What Affordable Housing options are available in Sonoma?



There are a number of affordable housing options available in Sonoma County. The county offers a variety of affordable housing programs that provide safe, decent, and affordable housing to low- and moderate-income households. The County’s Housing and Community Development Department administers the programs and works with a network of nonprofit housing developers and landlords.

The County’s affordable housing programs include:

The Housing Choice Voucher Program, which provides rental assistance to low-income households;

The Inclusionary Housing Program, which requires that a certain percentage of units in new developments be made available at below-market rates;

The Below Market Rate (BMR) Housing Program, which offers for-sale and for-rent units at below-market rates to households that meet income and other eligibility requirements;

The Farmworker Housing Program, which provides housing for farmworkers and their families;

The Senior Housing Program, which offers affordable housing options for seniors; and

The Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) Program, which allows for the construction of secondary units on single-family lots.
