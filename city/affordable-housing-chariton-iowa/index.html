The History of Affordable Housing in America



The history of affordable housing in America is a long and complicated one. Affordable housing has been an issue since the country was founded, and the debate over what to do about it has been ongoing ever since.

The first affordable housing program in America was created in 1937, during the Great Depression. The program, known as the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), provided loans to developers who were building housing for low- and moderate-income families. The FHA helped to finance the construction of millions of homes during the Depression and the postwar years.
