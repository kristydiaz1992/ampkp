What are the different types of affordable housing available in California?


There are many types of affordable housing available in California. Some of the most common types are:

1. Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) – LIHTC is a federal tax credit that helps developers build and rehabilitate affordable rental housing for low-income households.

2. Project-Based Section 8 – Project-based Section 8 is a HUD program that provides rental assistance to low-income households.

3. Housing Choice Voucher Program – The Housing Choice Voucher program, also known as “Section 8”, is a HUD program that provides rental assistance to low-income households.

4. Public Housing – Public housing is housing that is owned and operated by a public housing authority. Public housing is typically reserved for low-income households.

5. HUD-Insured Multifamily Housing – HUD-insured multifamily housing is housing that is insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA). This type of housing is typically reserved for low-income households.
