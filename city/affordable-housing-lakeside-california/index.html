The Benefits of Affordable Housing


There are many benefits to affordable housing, including reducing homelessness, providing stability for families and individuals, and creating economic opportunities.

Homelessness is a significant problem in the United States, and affordable housing can be a powerful tool in addressing it. According to the National Alliance to End Homelessness, there were nearly 600,000 people experiencing homelessness in the United States in 2017. Of those, nearly 40 percent were living in unsheltered locations, such as on the streets or in cars.

Affordable housing can provide a stable home for families and individuals who are struggling to make ends meet. According to the National Low Income Housing Coalition, more than 11 million extremely low-income households (those with incomes at or below 30 percent of the area median income) spend more than half of their income on housing. This can make it difficult to afford other necessities, such as food, clothing, and transportation.

Affordable housing can also create economic opportunities. According to the National Housing Conference, every $1 invested in affordable housing generates $4 in economic activity. This is because construction and maintenance of affordable housing creates jobs, and residents of affordable housing typically have more disposable income to spend on goods and services.

In sum, affordable housing is a critical part of addressing homelessness and poverty in the United States. It provides stability for families and individuals, and creates economic opportunities.
