The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, each with its own unique set of benefits and drawbacks. Here are some of the most common types of affordable housing:

Public Housing: Public housing is government-owned and -operated housing for low-income families. Public housing is usually managed by local housing authorities. Benefits of public housing include low rents and a wide variety of housing types and sizes. Drawbacks of public housing include long waiting lists and often poor maintenance.

Section 8 Housing: Section 8 housing is privately-owned housing that is subsidized by the government. Families who qualify for Section 8 assistance pay a portion of their income towards rent, with the government paying the remainder. Benefits of Section 8 housing include low rents and the ability to choose from a wide variety of housing options. Drawbacks of Section 8 housing include the fact that units are often in poor condition and that landlords can be difficult to work with.

Housing Choice Vouchers: Housing choice vouchers are a type of government assistance that helps families pay for rent in the private market. Families who qualify for housing vouchers receive a subsidy that they can use to help pay their rent. Benefits of housing choice vouchers include the ability to choose your own housing and the potential for longer-term assistance. Drawbacks of housing choice vouchers include the fact that they can be difficult to use and that they often do not cover the full cost of rent.

Low-Income Housing Tax Credits: The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) is a federal program that provides tax credits to developers who build or rehabilitate housing for low-income families. The LIHTC program is administered by state housing agencies. Benefits of the LIHTC program include the production of new affordable housing units and the rehabilitation of existing units. Drawbacks of the LIHTC program include the fact that it is often difficult to use the tax credits and that the units produced are often of poor quality.

Community Land Trusts: Community land trusts are nonprofit organizations that develop and manage affordable housing. Community land trusts often use a variety of financing sources to develop and operate their housing. Benefits of community land trusts include the production of new affordable housing units and the ability to control the long-term affordability of the units. Drawbacks of community land trusts include the fact that they often have difficulty acquiring funding and that they can be slow to develop new housing.

There are many other types of affordable housing, each with its own unique set of benefits and drawbacks. The best type of housing for a family depends on the family's needs and preferences.
