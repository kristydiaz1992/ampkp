One is that, as The Economist article points out, “land is getting scarce” in many parts of the country as the population continues to grow. Another factor is that, as The New York Times article notes, “construction costs have soared,” due in part to the rising price of land and the increased cost of labor and materials. This has made it more difficult for developers to build new housing that is affordable for low- and moderate-income families.

Finally, as The Economist article notes, “rents have been rising faster than incomes.” This is due to a number of factors, including the scarcity of affordable housing and the increased cost of living in many parts of the country.

The challenges of affordable housing are significant, but there are a number of possible solutions.
