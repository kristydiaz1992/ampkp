The Benefits of Affordable Housing for Seniors


As we age, our housing needs change. We may downsize to a smaller home or need to move into an assisted living facility. Regardless of our living situation, we all want to age with dignity and be able to remain in our own homes for as long as possible.

For many seniors, the high cost of housing is a major barrier to staying in their homes. According to a report by the National Low Income Housing Coalition, the “average senior citizen lives on a fixed income that is only about one-third of the median income for working-age households.”

Fortunately, there are a number of federal, state, and local programs that provide affordable housing for seniors. These programs can help seniors stay in their homes longer and maintain their independence.

The Section 202 program is a federally funded program that provides housing for low-income seniors. The program funds the construction of senior housing developments and provides subsidies to help low-income seniors afford their rent.

The Section 811 program is another federal program that provides housing for low-income seniors with disabilities. The program provides subsidies to help disabled seniors afford their rent and access to supportive services.

The Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) is a federal tax credit that incentivizes the development of affordable housing. The tax credit is available to developers of low-income housing developments.

The HOME Investment Partnerships Program (HOME) is a federal program that provides grants to states and localities to fund the construction of affordable housing developments.

The Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program is a federal program that provides grants to states and localities to fund a variety of community development activities, including the construction of affordable housing developments.

The Housing Choice Voucher program (formerly known as Section 8) is a federal program that provides subsidies to low-income households to help them afford their rent. Eligible households can receive a voucher that can be used to rent a unit in the private market.

The National Housing Trust Fund (NHTF) is a federal program that provides grants to states and localities to fund the construction of affordable housing developments for low-income households.

These are just a few of the many federal, state, and local programs that provide affordable housing for seniors. These programs can help seniors stay in their homes longer and maintain their independence.
