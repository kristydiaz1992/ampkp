The Different Types of Affordable Housing Available in Fort Lupton


There are many types of affordable housing available in Fort Lupton, Colorado. Below are just a few:

1. Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) – LIHTC is a federal tax credit that helps developers build and rehabilitate affordable rental housing for people with low incomes. This tax credit is available for both new construction and rehabilitation projects.

2. Housing Choice Vouchers (HCV) – HCV is a program that helps low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities afford decent and safe housing. Families who receive HCV can choose any housing that meets program requirements, including single-family homes, townhouses, and apartments.

3. Public Housing – Public housing is owned and operated by local public housing authorities. Public housing units are available to low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities.

4. Section 8 Project-Based Rental Assistance (PBRA) – PBRA is a program that provides rental assistance for low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities. PBRA units are located in specific housing developments and are owned and operated by private landlords.

5. Section 811 Project-Based Rental Assistance for Persons with Disabilities – Section 811 is a program that provides rental assistance for low-income persons with disabilities. Section 811 units are located in specific housing developments and are owned and operated by private landlords.
