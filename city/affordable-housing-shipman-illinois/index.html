The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, and each type has its own benefits and drawbacks. Below, we'll take a look at some of the most common types of affordable housing so you can decide which one is right for you.

Public Housing: Public housing is housing that is owned and operated by the government. Public housing is usually reserved for low-income families and individuals, although there are some public housing units that are available to middle-class families as well. Public housing units are typically much less expensive than units in the private market, but they are also usually smaller and in less desirable locations.

Section 8 Housing: Section 8 housing is a program that provides subsidies to low-income families so they can afford to rent units in the private market. The subsidy is paid directly to the landlord, and the family is responsible for paying the difference between the rent and the subsidy. Section 8 housing is often criticized because it can lead to families being priced out of desirable neighborhoods.

Housing Choice Vouchers: Housing choice vouchers are similar to Section 8 housing, but they can be used to rent units in any neighborhood, not just low-income neighborhoods. Housing choice vouchers are also portable, so families can move to any area where they can find an affordable unit.

Low-Income Housing Tax Credits: The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) is a federal program that provides tax credits to developers who build or rehabilitate affordable housing units. Developers can sell the tax credits to investors to raise money for their projects. The LIHTC is the most common source of financing for affordable housing projects.

Community Land Trusts: Community land trusts are nonprofit organizations that develop and manage affordable housing. Community land trusts typically own the land on which the housing is built, and they sell or lease the units to low- and moderate-income families. Community land trusts can be an effective way to keep housing affordable in gentrifying areas.

There are many other types of affordable housing, but these are some of the most common. Each type of affordable housing has its own advantages and disadvantages, so it's important to do your research before you decide which one is right for you.
