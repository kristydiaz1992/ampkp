7 ways to make affordable housing work in your community


There’s no one-size-fits-all solution to making affordable housing work in your community, but there are a few key things that can make a big difference.

1. Get everyone on the same page

Making affordable housing work starts with getting everyone on the same page. That means elected officials, city staff, developers, and community members all need to be aware of the issue and committed to finding a solution.

2. Define what “affordable housing” means in your community

One of the first steps is to define what “affordable housing” means in your community. This will help ensure that everyone is on the same page about what the goals are and what types of housing are included in the definition.

3. Create a dedicated funding source for affordable housing

One of the biggest challenges to creating affordable housing is finding the funding to pay for it. One way to address this is to create a dedicated funding source for affordable housing. This could be a tax on vacant properties, a fee on new development, or a dedicated portion of the city’s annual budget.

4. Encourage mixed-income housing

One way to make affordable housing work better in your community is to encourage mixed-income housing. This means creating housing that includes a mix of incomes, so that people of different economic backgrounds can live in the same neighborhood.

5. Make it easier to build accessory dwelling units

Making it easier to build accessory dwelling units (ADUs) is another way to create more affordable housing. ADUs are small living spaces that can be built on an existing property, such as a house or an apartment.

6. Streamline the permitting process

The permitting process for new construction can be a barrier to creating affordable housing. One way to address this is to streamline the permitting process, so that it is simpler and faster to get approvals for new projects.

7. Get creative with zoning

Zoning can be a major barrier to creating affordable housing. One way to address this is to get creative with zoning, so that more types of housing are allowed in more areas. This could include allowing tiny homes, ADUs, and other types of housing in areas that are currently zoned for single-family homes.

What is Affordable Housing?

Affordable housing is housing that is available at a price that is affordable to low- and moderate-income households. It’s often defined as housing that costs no more than 30% of a household’s monthly income.
