The difference between affordable housing and low income housing


There is a big difference between affordable housing and low income housing. Affordable housing is housing that is priced so that a person or family can pay for it without spending more than 30% of their income on housing costs. This includes rent or a mortgage, property taxes, and utilities. Low income housing is housing that is earmarked for people or families who earn below a certain income level. In the United States, this is usually 60% of the median income for the area. Low income housing is often subsidized by the government, which means that the government helps to pay for part or all of the rent or mortgage.
