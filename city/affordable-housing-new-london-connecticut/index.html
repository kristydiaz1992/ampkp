1. Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC)

Pros:
-Can be used to finance the construction or rehabilitation of low-income housing
- Provides an incentive for developers to build affordable housing

Cons:
- Requires a lengthy and complicated application process
-May not be available in all areas

2. Housing Choice Vouchers

Pros:
-Assists low-income families with the cost of housing
-Can be used to rent homes from private landlords or units in public or subsidized housing developments

Cons:
-There is often a long waiting list for voucher assistance
-Voucher amounts may not be enough to cover the full cost of rent

3. Section 8 Project-Based Rental Assistance

Pros:
- Provides financial assistance to low-income renters
-Can be used to rent units in specific developments or buildings

Cons:
- Requires the approval of a local public housing agency
- May be difficult to find available units

4. Public Housing

Pros:
- Provides affordable housing for low-income families
- Rents are based on income

Cons:
- Availability is limited
-May have long waiting lists
- Units may not be well-maintained
