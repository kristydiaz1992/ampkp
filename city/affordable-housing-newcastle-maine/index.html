5 ways to make affordable housing work in your town


There is no one-size-fits-all solution to the affordable housing crisis, but there are some things that cities and towns can do to make it work for them. Here are five ideas:

1. Accessory dwelling units: Allow homeowners to build small, secondary units on their property. This can provide affordable housing options without changing the character of the neighborhood.

2. Density bonuses: Give developers bonuses for including affordable housing units in their projects. This can help to offset the higher costs of building these units.

3. Inclusionary zoning: Require that a certain percentage of new housing units be designated as affordable. This can ensure that new development includes a mix of housing options.

4. Tax breaks: Provide tax breaks or other financial incentives to developers or homeowners who provide affordable housing units.

5. Education and outreach: Educate the community about the importance of affordable housing and work with them to find solutions that work for your town.
