10 ways to make affordable housing work in Truckee, California


The problem of affordable housing is not new to the town of Truckee, California. In fact, the issue has been a topic of concern and debate for many years. As the cost of living and housing prices continue to rise, it is becoming increasingly difficult for working families and individuals to find affordable housing options.

The town of Truckee is committed to finding solutions to the affordable housing problem and has been working with various stakeholders to identify and implement strategies that will make affordable housing more accessible and attainable for all.

Here are 10 ways to make affordable housing work in Truckee, California:

1. Establish an affordable housing trust fund: One way to generate revenue for affordable housing is to establish an affordable housing trust fund. This fund could be used to provide grants and loans to developers for the construction or rehabilitation of affordable housing units.

2. Incentivize the development of workforce housing: Another way to encourage the development of affordable housing is to provide incentives for developers who build workforce housing. These incentives could include density bonuses, reduced development fees, or fast-track approval processes.

3. Require inclusionary zoning: Inclusionary zoning is a policy that requires developers to set aside a certain percentage of units in a new development for low- and moderate-income households. By requiring developers to include affordable units in their projects, more affordable housing units will be built overall.

4. Make use of vacant and underutilized land: There is a lot of vacant and underutilized land in the town of Truckee that could be used for the development of affordable housing. One way to do this is through a land banking program, where the town purchases vacant land and makes it available for affordable housing development.

5. Increase funding for affordable housing programs: Another way to address the affordable housing issue is to increase funding for existing affordable housing programs. This could be done through public-private partnerships or by increasing taxes or fees on things like short-term rentals.

6. Allow for accessory dwelling units: Accessory dwelling units (ADUs) are small homes that are built on the same property as a primary residence. They can be used as rental units, guest houses, or even primary residences. Allowing for ADUs can help to increase the supply of affordable housing.

7. Encourage the development of micro-units: Micro-units are small living spaces, usually around 300 square feet or less. They are a type of housing that is becoming increasingly popular in cities where space is limited and housing prices are high. By encouraging the development of micro-units, more affordable housing options will be available.

8. Implement a housing first policy: A housing first policy prioritizes getting people into permanent housing before addressing other issues like job training or addiction counseling. This approach has been shown to be effective in reducing homelessness and could help to increase the number of people who have access to affordable housing.

9. Create a community land trust: A community land trust is a nonprofit organization that owns land and develops it for community benefit. This could include developing affordable housing or developing land for community gardens or parks. Community land trusts can help to ensure that land is used for the benefit of the community, rather than for private profit.

10. Advocate for state and federal funding: One way to make sure that affordable housing is a priority for government funding is to advocate for more state and federal funding for affordable housing programs. This could be done through legislation, ballot initiatives, or public education campaigns.
