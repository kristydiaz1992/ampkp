1. Increase Low-Income Housing Tax Credits

2. Expand the Earned Income Tax Credit

The Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) is a refundable tax credit for low- and moderate-income working families. The credit reduces the amount of taxes owed and can result in a refund for families who qualify.

3. Implement Inclusionary Zoning

Inclusionary zoning is a land-use planning tool that requires or encourages the production of affordable housing in new development. When inclusionary zoning policies are implemented, a portion of the units in a new development must be set aside for low- or moderate-income households.

4. Invest in Housing Counseling

Housing counseling services help families navigate the often-complex world of housing. Services can include help with finding an affordable place to live, understanding and navigating the eviction process, and accessing government benefits.

5. Increase Funding for Housing Assistance Programs

Housing assistance programs like Section 8 and public housing provide rental assistance to low- and moderate-income families. These programs are vital to helping families afford safe and stable housing.

7. Establish a Housing Trust Fund

A housing trust fund is a dedicated source of revenue for affordable housing. The fund can be used to finance the construction and preservation of affordable housing, provide rental assistance, and support other initiatives to increase access to affordable housing.
