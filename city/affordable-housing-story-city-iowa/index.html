Ideas for increasing affordable housing in Story City, Iowa


There is no one silver bullet for increasing affordable housing, but here are a few ideas for Story City, Iowa:

1. Create a housing trust fund: A housing trust fund can be used to finance the construction or rehabilitation of affordable housing units. The fund can be created through public-private partnerships, local government appropriations, or philanthropic donations.

2. Incentivize developers to include affordable housing: Local governments can offer developers density bonuses, reduced fees, or other incentives to include a certain percentage of affordable units in their projects.

3. Zoning for inclusionary housing: Zoning ordinances can require that a certain percentage of units in a new development are affordable to low- or moderate-income households.

4. Require affordable units in government-funded projects: When a local government is funding a new housing development, it can require that a certain percentage of units be set aside for low- or moderate-income households.

5. Create community land trusts: Community land trusts are nonprofits that own land and develop or manage housing on that land. The land is leased to households, rather than sold, so it remains affordable in perpetuity.

6. Make use of vacant and foreclosed properties: Properties that are vacant or in foreclosure can be converted into affordable housing units through public-private partnerships or other innovative financing mechanisms.

7. Increase funding for affordable housing: Local, state, and federal governments can increase funding for affordable housing initiatives through appropriations, tax credits, or other means.

8. Encourage homeownership: Homeownership is one way to increase housing affordability, as it allows families to build equity and eventually pay off their mortgage. Local governments can encourage homeownership through down payment assistance programs, mortgage counseling, and other initiatives.

9. Promote housing choice vouchers: Housing choice vouchers help low-income households rent units in the private market. The voucher program can be promoted by local governments through public education campaigns and outreach to landlords.

10. Support nonprofit organizations that develop and manage affordable housing: Local governments can support nonprofit organizations that are involved in developing and managing affordable housing units. This support can take the form of financial assistance, in-kind resources, or technical assistance.
