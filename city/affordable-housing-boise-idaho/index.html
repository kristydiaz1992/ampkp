4. More money available for other necessities, such as food and clothing.

5. A smaller environmental footprint, as people are able to live in smaller homes or apartments.

6. A strengthened sense of community, as people are brought together by their shared experience of living in affordable housing.

The Impact of Affordable Housing on the Economy of Boise


There is no denying that affordable housing is one of the most important issues facing the City of Boise today. A recent study by the National Low Income Housing Coalition found that, on average, renters in Boise need to earn $17.50 per hour to afford a modest two-bedroom apartment - that's almost double the federal minimum wage of $7.25.

With such a high cost of living, it's no wonder that many families are struggling to make ends meet. What's more, the issue of affordable housing is not just affecting low-income families - it's also having a major impact on the local economy.

When families can't afford to live in decent housing, they are forced to spend less money on other things, like food, clothing, and transportation. This, in turn, leads to less money being circulated throughout the local economy, which can eventually lead to job losses and a decline in business activity.

The City of Boise is currently working on a number of initiatives to address the affordable housing crisis, including increasing funding for affordable housing programs and working with the private sector to create more affordable housing options.

It's clear that the issue of affordable housing is having a major impact on the economy of Boise. And, while there is no easy solution, it's important that we continue to work together to find ways to address this critical issue.
