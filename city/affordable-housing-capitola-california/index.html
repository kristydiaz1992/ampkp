What is the definition of affordable housing?

The Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) defines affordable housing as "safe and decent housing that is affordable to low- and moderate-income households." Affordable housing includes a variety of housing types, such as public housing, privately-owned rental housing, and owner-occupied housing.

What is the difference between public housing and affordable housing?

Public housing is a type of affordable housing that is owned and operated by government entities, such as local housing authorities. Public housing units are typically reserved for low-income households. Affordable housing, on the other hand, can be owned and operated by both public and private entities. While affordable housing units may also be reserved for low-income households, they are not required to be.

What are the income limits for affordable housing?

Income limits for affordable housing vary depending on the type of housing and the location. HUD publishes income limits for all states and counties on its website.

What is the difference between rent-restricted and income-restricted housing?

Rent-restricted housing units have maximum rents that are set below the market rate. Income-restricted housing units, on the other hand, are only available to households that meet certain income criteria.

No, you do not have to be a U.S. citizen to qualify for affordable housing. However, you may need to meet other requirements, such as having a certain income level or being a part of a targeted population group.
