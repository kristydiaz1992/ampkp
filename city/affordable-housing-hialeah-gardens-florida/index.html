Inclusionary zoning and affordable housing


Inclusionary zoning is a type of zoning that requires a certain percentage of housing units in a development to be designated as affordable housing. The specific requirements vary from one jurisdiction to another, but the goal is to create mixed-income developments that include a mix of market-rate and affordable units.

There are a number of benefits to inclusionary zoning. By creating more affordable housing units, it can help to address the issue of housing affordability. It can also help to create more diverse and vibrant communities, as well as improve access to essential services and amenities.

However, inclusionary zoning can also create challenges for developers, who may need to find ways to offset the costs of including affordable units in their developments. In some cases, this can lead to taller and/or denser development, as well as a greater reliance on subsidies.

Nonetheless, inclusionary zoning can be a powerful tool for creating more affordable housing and promoting socioeconomic diversity.
