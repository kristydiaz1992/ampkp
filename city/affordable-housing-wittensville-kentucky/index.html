Public housing is housing that is owned and managed by a government agency, typically at the local or state level. Public housing is usually reserved for low-income households, although there may be some exceptions. One benefit of public housing is that it is often more affordable than other types of housing. However, public housing can also be less safe and secure than other types of housing, and there may be waiting lists for units.

Section 8 housing is a type of housing assistance that is provided by the federal government. Households that qualify for Section 8 assistance receive a voucher that they can use to help pay for rent. Section 8 housing can be an affordable option for low-income households, but there may be a limited supply of units available.

Low income housing tax credit (LIHTC) properties are privately-owned properties that receive a tax credit from the federal government in exchange for renting units to low-income households. LIHTC properties typically have income restrictions, meaning that only households that earn below a certain amount can qualify for units. One benefit of LIHTC properties is that they are usually high-quality and well-maintained. However, LIHTC properties can also be more expensive than other types of housing, and there may be waiting lists for units.

Housing choice vouchers (HCVs) are another type of housing assistance that is provided by the federal government. HCVs are also known as “Section 8 vouchers.” Households that qualify for HCVs receive a voucher that they can use to help pay for rent. HCVs can be used to help households rent units in the private market, and can be an affordable option for low-income households. However, HCVs can also be difficult to use, and there may be a limited supply of units available.
