7 ways to make affordable housing work in your city


There is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how to make affordable housing work in your city, but there are a number of strategies that can help.

1. Incentivize developers to build affordable housing.

One way to encourage the development of affordable housing is to offer developers incentives to include a certain percentage of affordable units in their projects. These can include things like density bonuses, reduced parking requirements, or tax breaks.

2. Require inclusionary zoning.

Inclusionary zoning is a planning tool that requires a certain percentage of new development be dedicated to affordable housing. This can help to ensure that new growth benefits the entire community, not just those who can afford to pay top dollar for housing.

3. Invest in public housing.

Public housing is a vital part of the affordable housing stock in many cities, and it is important to invest in its upkeep and improvement. This can include things like making energy-efficiency upgrades or providing supportive services for residents.

4. Create land banks.

Land banks are publicly-owned parcels of land that can be used for affordable housing development. This can help to ensure that there is adequate land available for such development, and that it is not being held up by private developers for speculative purposes.

5. Increase funding for affordable housing.

One way to make affordable housing a priority is to increase funding for it at the local, state, or federal level. This can be used to create or preserve affordable units, or to provide rental assistance or other support to low-income households.

6. Advocate for fair housing.

It is important to advocate for fair housing laws and policies at all levels of government. This includes things like ensuring that zoning ordinances do not discriminate against low-income housing developments and opposing efforts to weaken fair housing protections.

7. Educate the public about affordable housing.

Finally, it is important to educate the public about the importance of affordable housing and dispel common myths and misconceptions about it. This can help to build support for affordable housing initiatives and ensure that they are seen as a positive force in the community.

How to make affordable housing more sustainable


According to a report by the National Low Income Housing Coalition, 56 percent of extremely low income households—those earning 30 percent or less of the area median income—in the United States pay more than half of their incomes on rent. For these families, finding affordable housing is a never-ending struggle, and one that is exacerbated by a number of factors, including the increasing cost of living, stagnant wages, and the lack of affordable housing options.

There are a number of ways to make affordable housing more sustainable, including:

1. Increasing funding for affordable housing initiatives

One way to make affordable housing more sustainable is to increase funding for affordable housing initiatives. This could include increasing funding for programs like the Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC), which provides tax credits to developers who build or rehabilitate affordable housing.

2. Encouraging mixed-income development

Another way to make affordable housing more sustainable is to encourage mixed-income development, which refers to developments that include a mix of housing units that are affordable to different income levels. This can help to ensure that there is a mix of incomes within a community, which can help to support local businesses and make the community more economically diverse.

3. Creating incentives for sustainable building practices

One way to make sustainable affordable housing more prevalent is to create incentives for sustainable building practices. This could include offering tax breaks or other financial incentives to developers who build energy-efficient and water-efficient affordable housing units.

4. Promoting sustainable transportation options

Another way to make affordable housing more sustainable is to promote sustainable transportation options, such as public transportation, walking, and biking. This can help to reduce the reliance on cars, which can help to reduce traffic congestion and air pollution.

5. Requiring green building standards for affordable housing

One way to make sustainable affordable housing the norm is to require green building standards for all new affordable housing developments. This could include requiring that developments meet certain standards for energy efficiency, water efficiency, and indoor air quality.

Making affordable housing more sustainable is a complex issue, and one that will require a multi-faceted approach. However, by taking steps like those listed above, we can start to make a difference and create more sustainable, affordable housing options for all.
