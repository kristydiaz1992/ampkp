The Different Types of Affordable Housing in California


There are many different types of affordable housing in California. Below are some of the most common types:

1. Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) housing: This type of affordable housing is usually developed and managed by private companies or non-profit organizations. The developers receive tax credits from the government in exchange for providing affordable housing to low-income households.

2. Public housing: This type of affordable housing is owned and operated by government agencies. Public housing is usually reserved for low-income households.

3. Section 8 housing: This type of affordable housing is privately owned but the government subsidizes the rent for low-income households.

4. HUD housing: This type of affordable housing is owned by the government and is available to low-income households, senior citizens, and disabled people.

5. Housing Choice Vouchers: Also known as "Section 8 vouchers", this program provides low-income households with vouchers that can be used to rent housing from private landlords.
