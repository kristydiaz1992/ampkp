5 Ways to Improve Affordable Housing in Highland Township


There is no one-size-fits-all solution to the affordable housing crisis, but here are five ways that Highland Township can improve affordable housing options for residents:

One way to incentivize developers to build more affordable housing is to offer density bonuses, which allow them to build more units on a given parcel of land than would normally be allowed. This can help make development projects more financially feasible for builders, and ultimately lead to more affordable housing options for residents.

2. Create a community land trust.

Community land trusts are organizations that purchase land and then lease it to homeowners or developers with the intention of keeping the property affordable in perpetuity. This can help ensure that there is a stock of affordable housing in the community that is not at risk of being gentrified or redeveloped.

3. Require inclusionary zoning.

Inclusionary zoning is a planning tool that requires a certain percentage of units in new developments to be set aside for low- or moderate-income households. This can help create a mix of incomes and help to prevent neighborhoods from becoming too homogeneous.

4. Make use of public land.

Highland Township can make use of public land to create new affordable housing developments. This land can either be leased or sold to developers at a discounted rate, with the stipulation that the units built on the site must be affordable.

5. Increase funding for affordable housing initiatives.

The township can increase funding for affordable housing initiatives by allocating more money to the Highland Township Housing Commission or by establishing a dedicated affordable housing fund. This additional funding can be used to create or preserve affordable housing units, provide down payment assistance, or fund other initiatives that make housing more affordable for residents.
