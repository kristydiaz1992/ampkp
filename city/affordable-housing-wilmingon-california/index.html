5 ways to make affordable housing a reality in Wilmington, CA


There is no one answer to the affordable housing crisis, but there are a number of things that can be done to make housing more affordable in Wilmington, CA.

1. Establish an inclusionary zoning ordinance

Inclusionary zoning ordinances require that a certain percentage of units in new developments be set aside for low- or moderate-income households. This can help to increase the supply of affordable housing and ensure that new development is accessible to a range of income levels.

2. Create a community land trust

Community land trusts are organizations that hold land in trust for the benefit of the community. They can be used to develop and preserve affordable housing, as well as to provide other community benefits such as parks and community gardens.

3. Increase funding for affordable housing

There is a significant need for investment in affordable housing. Local, state, and federal governments can all play a role in increasing funding for affordable housing initiatives.

4. Encourage accessory dwelling units

Accessory dwelling units (ADUs) are small living units that are attached to or located on the same property as a single-family home. They can provide an affordable housing option for families, students, and others.

5. Improve public transportation

Improving public transportation can make it easier for people to access affordable housing options that are located further away from downtown or other expensive areas. This can help to reduce the overall cost of living for low- and moderate-income households.
