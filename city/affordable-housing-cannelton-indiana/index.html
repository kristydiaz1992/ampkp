The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, each with its own benefits and drawbacks. The most common types of affordable housing are public housing, Section 8 housing, and Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) housing.

Public housing is housing that is owned and operated by the government. Public housing is typically the most affordable housing option, but it can be difficult to get into as there is often high demand and waiting lists can be lengthy. Public housing can also be of lower quality than other types of housing, as it is often not well-maintained by the government.

Section 8 housing is housing that is subsidized by the government. Section 8 housing is usually of better quality than public housing, as the government does not own or operate the property. Instead, the government provides a subsidy to the landlord, which reduces the rent that the tenant has to pay. Section 8 housing can be difficult to get into as there is often high demand and waiting lists can be lengthy.

Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) housing is housing that is developed and operated with the help of tax credits. LIHTC housing is typically of good quality and is affordable for low-income households. However, LIHTC housing can be difficult to get into as there is often high demand and waiting lists can be lengthy.
