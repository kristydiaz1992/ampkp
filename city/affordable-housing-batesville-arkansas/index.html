What is Affordable Housing and Who Does It Benefit?


Affordable housing is defined as housing that costs no more than 30% of a household’s income. This can be tricky to define because income varies greatly from place to place and family to family. In order to make the definition more uniform, many government and private organizations use the Area Median Income (AMI) as a way to standardize who is and is not eligible for affordable housing.

There are a number of different types of affordable housing, each with their own benefits. For example, Low-Income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC) are a type of affordable housing that is available to people who make below 60% of the AMI. These tax credits can be used to help offset the cost of rent or to help with the down payment on a home.

Another type of affordable housing is Public Housing. Public housing is owned and operated by the government and is available to people who make below 80% of the AMI. Because public housing is government-run, it is often very affordable and can be a good option for people who are struggling to find housing that they can afford.
