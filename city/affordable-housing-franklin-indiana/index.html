Innovative financing models for affordable housing


There is a growing recognition that the traditional model of affordable housing financing – relying heavily on government subsidies – is not working. In response, a number of innovative financing models are being developed that seek to attract private investment into the affordable housing sector.

One such model is the Housing Investment Trust (HIT), a non-profit entity that pools private capital from a range of sources to invest in affordable housing projects. The HIT then provides loans or equity investments to developers to help them build or preserve affordable housing.

Another innovative financing model is the Social Impact Bond (SIB). A SIB is a contract between a government body and a private investor, under which the investor agrees to provide funding for a social program. If the program achieves certain predetermined outcomes, the government pays the investor back with interest.

The SIB model is still in its early stages, but it has the potential to attract significant private investment into the affordable housing sector.

The HIT and SIB models are just two of the many innovative financing models that are being developed to attract private investment into affordable housing. As the need for affordable housing continues to grow, it is likely that more and more of these models will be put into use.
