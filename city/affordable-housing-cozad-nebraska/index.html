3 affordable housing developments that are changing lives


Making housing more affordable has always been a top priority for lawmakers and policy makers. In recent years, there have been a number of innovative and effective affordable housing developments that are changing lives. Here are three of them:

1. The Community Land Trust Model

The Community Land Trust (CLT) model is a new way of developing and owning affordable housing. CLTs are typically set up as non-profit organizations and they work to acquire land and then develop it for housing. The housing that is built on CLT land is then sold or leased to income-qualified households at an affordable price. One of the key advantages of the CLT model is that it can help to keep housing affordable in areas that are experiencing gentrification.

2. Housing Cooperatives

Housing cooperatives are another type of innovative affordable housing development. Cooperatives are typically owned and managed by their members, who are also the occupants of the housing. Members of a cooperative typically have a say in how the property is managed and they share in the responsibilities of maintaining the property. Housing cooperatives can be an effective way to provide affordable housing, especially in high-cost areas.

3. Accessory Dwelling Units

Accessory dwelling units (ADUs) are small living quarters that are attached to or located on the same property as a single-family home. ADUs can be an effective way to add more affordable housing units to an existing neighborhood. They can also provide homeowners with additional income by renting out the unit.

These are just a few of the many innovative and effective affordable housing developments that are changing lives.
