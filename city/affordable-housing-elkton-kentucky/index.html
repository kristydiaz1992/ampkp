The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, each with their own unique benefits and drawbacks. Here are some of the most common types of affordable housing:

1. Public housing is housing that is owned and managed by the government. It is typically reserved for low-income families and individuals. One of the main benefits of public housing is that it is very affordable. However, public housing can also be cramped and in poor repair.

2. Subsidized housing is housing that is subsidized by the government. This type of housing is typically reserved for low-income families and individuals. One of the main benefits of subsidized housing is that it is much more affordable than market-rate housing. However, subsidized housing can also be in poor repair and may not be located in desirable areas.

3. Senior housing is housing that is reserved for seniors, typically those who are 55 years of age or older. Senior housing can be either subsidized or market-rate. One of the main benefits of senior housing is that it is typically very well-maintained. However, senior housing can also be more expensive than other types of housing.

4. Manufactured housing is housing that is built in a factory and then transported to a site. Manufactured housing is typically more affordable than traditional site-built housing. However, manufactured housing can be less energy-efficient and may not be as durable as site-built housing.

5. Accessory dwelling units (ADUs) are small living spaces that are attached to or located on the same property as a single-family home. ADUs can be an affordable housing option for families and individuals. However, they may not provide as much privacy as a standalone unit.

6. Cohousing is a type of intentional community in which residents share common facilities and common space. Cohousing communities are typically very supportive and close-knit. However, they may not be suitable for everyone, as they require a high level of involvement from residents.
