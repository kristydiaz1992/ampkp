The History of Affordable Housing


Affordable housing has been a goal of social activists and policymakers for centuries. In the United States, the first affordable housing program was created in 1934 as part of the New Deal. The program, known as the Federal Housing Administration, provided loans to developers who built homes for low- and moderate-income families.

The FHA helped to spur the growth of the suburban housing market, but it did not do much to create truly affordable housing for the poorest Americans. In the 1950s, the government began to build public housing developments in urban areas. These developments were meant to provide safe, clean, and affordable housing for low-income families. However, many of these developments became crime-ridden and were not well-maintained by the government.

In the 1970s and 1980s, the federal government began to deregulate the housing market and to cut funding for affordable housing programs. This led to a decrease in the number of affordable housing units and an increase in homelessness. In the 1990s, the government began to invest more in affordable housing programs, but the number of units produced has not kept up with the demand.

The need for affordable housing is as great as ever. In many cities, the cost of housing has increased faster than the cost of living, making it difficult for low- and moderate-income families to find places to live. The federal government, state governments, and local governments are all working to create more affordable housing, but the need still far outstrips the supply.

How to Qualify for Affordable Housing


There is a common misconception that affordable housing is only available to low-income households. However, this is not the case. Affordable housing is available to anyone who meets the income and asset guidelines set forth by the government.

In order to qualify for affordable housing, you must first meet the income guidelines. These guidelines vary from state to state, but in general, you must make less than 80% of the median income in your area. For example, if the median income in your town is $50,000, you would need to make less than $40,000 in order to qualify.

In addition to income, you must also have a good credit score and a steady job. You will also need to prove that you have the ability to pay rent. Most affordable housing units require that you pay no more than 30% of your income in rent.

If you think you may qualify for affordable housing, the best way to find out is to contact your local housing authority. They will be able to help you determine if you qualify and connect you with available units in your area.
