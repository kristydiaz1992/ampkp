How to Solve Cottonwood's Affordable Housing Crisis


Cottonwood, like many small towns in the American West, is facing an affordable housing crisis. The town is rapidly growing, but the number of available homes is not keeping pace. As a result, prices are rising and many residents are being priced out of the market.

There are a number of ways to solve this problem, but it will require cooperation from the town's residents, businesses, and government.

One solution is to build more affordable housing. This can be done by zoning for more density, making it easier for developers to build multi-family homes and apartments. The town can also offer incentives for developers to build affordable housing, such as waiving fees or providing land.

Another solution is to create a community land trust. This would allow the town to purchase land and then lease it to developers or homeowners at below-market rates. The land would be permanently affordable and would remain in the trust even if the property changed hands.

Yet another solution is to make it easier for residents to build their own homes. This could be done by allowing accessory dwelling units (such as granny flats or in-law units) and by easing building code restrictions.

All of these solutions require a commitment from the town's residents, businesses, and government to solve the affordable housing crisis. But if Cottonwood is to remain a thriving community, it is essential that something is done.
