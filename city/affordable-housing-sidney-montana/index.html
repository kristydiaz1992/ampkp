The Different Types of Affordable Housing Available in Sidney


There are a variety of affordable housing options available in Sidney, British Columbia, including social housing, co-operative housing, and private sector rentals.

Social housing is housing that is owned and operated by the government or a non-profit organization. It is typically offered at below-market rates and is reserved for low-income households.

Co-operative housing is a type of housing where members own and manage the property together. Co-ops typically have lower monthly fees than traditional apartments, and members may also be required to work a certain number of hours per week on property maintenance.

Private sector rentals are apartments or houses that are rented out by private landlords. These units are typically not subsidized and the rent is set at market rates. However, there are often rent controls in place that limit how much landlords can increase rent from one year to the next.
