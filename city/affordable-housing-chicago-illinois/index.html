Affordable Housing In Chicago , Illinois

The Chicago Housing Authority: Promoting Affordable Housing in the Windy City


The Chicago Housing Authority (CHA) is a government organization that promotes affordable housing in the city of Chicago. The organization was created in 1937, during the Great Depression, to help address the city’s housing crisis. The CHA provides housing for low-income residents, as well as residents with special needs.

The CHA owns and manages several housing developments throughout the city. The organization also provides subsidies and assistance to private landlords who rent to low-income residents. In recent years, the CHA has been working to redevelop some of its older housing developments. This work includes demolishing old buildings and replacing them with new, mixed-income developments.

The CHA’s work has helped to improve the quality of life for many low-income residents in Chicago. The organization’s efforts have also been praised by housing advocates and city leaders.

Cook County's Housing Choice Voucher Program: A Path to Affordable Housing in Chicago


The Cook County Housing Choice Voucher Program (HCP) is a federally funded program that provides rental assistance to low-income families in Cook County, Illinois. The program is administered by the Cook County Department of Housing and is available to families who meet the income and eligibility guidelines.

The HCP program is designed to help families find and maintain affordable housing in the private market. Families who participate in the program receive a voucher that they can use to rent an apartment, house, or mobile home of their choice. The voucher covers a portion of the rent, and the family is responsible for paying the remainder.

The HCP program is a vital resource for low-income families in Cook County, as it provides them with a path to affordable housing. The program has helped thousands of families secure safe and affordable homes in Chicago and its suburbs.

If you are interested in learning more about the HCP program or applying for assistance, please contact the Cook County Department of Housing.

The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit: A Key Component of Affordable Housing in Chicago


The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) is a key component of affordable housing in Chicago. The LIHTC program is a federal tax credit that provides incentives for developers to create and rehabilitate affordable rental housing for low- and moderate-income families.

The LIHTC program was created in 1986 as part of the Tax Reform Act. The program is administered by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) and is available to developers of affordable rental housing. The LIHTC program is the largest source of federal funding for affordable housing.

In order to receive the LIHTC, developers must submit an application to the IRS. The application is then reviewed by the state housing agency. If the project is approved, the developer will receive a credit that can be used to offset federal taxes.

The LIHTC program has been successful in creating and preserving affordable rental housing in Chicago. As of 2016, there were over 1,700 LIHTC-supported rental units in Chicago. The program has also been successful in creating jobs and stimulating economic development.

The LIHTC program is an important tool for creating and preserving affordable housing in Chicago. The program has helped to create thousands of affordable rental units and has also stimulated economic development.

The City of Chicago's Affordable Housing Plan
