How Affordable Housing Impacts the Community


The term “affordable housing” is used to describe dwelling units that are affordable by people with a wide range of incomes. It is often used interchangeably with “low-income housing” or “subsidized housing.” Affordable housing can be found in both the private and public sectors, and can be provided through a variety of financing mechanisms.

The Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program is a federally funded program that provides grants to states, local governments, and nonprofit organizations for the purpose of developing affordable housing and improving neighborhoods. The program is administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD).

The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) program is a federal tax credit that incentivizes the development of affordable housing. The credit is provided to developers of low-income housing projects, who in turn pass the savings on to tenants in the form of lower rents.

The Public Housing program is a federally funded program that provides housing for low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities. Public housing is managed by local housing authorities, which are overseen by HUD.

The Section 8 program is a federally funded program that provides rental assistance to low-income families. The program is administered by HUD, and families receiving assistance are able to choose their own housing.
