The History of Affordable Housing

The history of affordable housing is long and complicated, but it can be traced back to the early 20th century. The first major federal legislation aimed at providing affordable housing was the National Housing Act of 1934. This act created the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), which provided insurance for mortgages. The FHA helped to spur the growth of the suburban housing market by making it easier for middle-class families to obtain loans for new homes.

The next major piece of legislation was the Housing and Community Development Act of 1974. This act created the Community Development Block Grant program, which provided funding for local governments to build affordable housing. The act also created the Housing Choice Voucher program, which helps low-income families afford rentals in the private market.

The most recent major piece of affordable housing legislation is the Housing and Economic Recovery Act of 2008. This act created the Tax Credit Exchange program, which provides tax credits to developers who build or rehabilitate affordable housing.
