5 ways to make affordable housing work in Alachua


There is no one-size-fits-all solution to the affordable housing crisis, but there are some things that cities can do to make affordable housing work for their residents. Here are five ways to make affordable housing work in Alachua:

1. Incentivize developers to build more affordable housing.

One way to encourage more affordable housing development is to offer incentives to developers who build units that are affordable to low- and moderate-income households. These can include density bonuses, fee waivers, or other financial incentives.

2. Create or expand inclusionary zoning requirements.

Inclusionary zoning requires that a certain percentage of units in a new development be set aside for low- and moderate-income households. This can help to ensure that new development includes a mix of housing options that are affordable to a range of households.

3. Require developers to contribute to an affordable housing fund.

Another way to generate funding for affordable housing is to require developers to contribute to an affordable housing fund as part of their project approval process. This can provide a dedicated source of funding that can be used to support the development or preservation of affordable housing.

4. Encourage the use of accessory dwelling units.

Accessory dwelling units (ADUs) are small, secondary units that can be built on a property that already has a primary residence. ADUs can provide an affordable housing option for low- and moderate-income households, and they can also help to increase the density of a neighborhood, which can make it more affordable for everyone.

5. Implement “ tiered” zoning codes.

Tiered zoning codes allow for a mix of housing types and densities in a single zoning district. This can help to create more affordable housing options by allowing for a mix of housing types, including smaller, more affordable units.
