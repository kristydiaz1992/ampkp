The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, but the most common are public housing, voucher-based housing, and tax credit housing.

Public housing is owned and operated by the government and is reserved for low-income families. Voucher-based housing is similar to public housing, but instead of being owned by the government, it is privately owned and the government provides subsidies to low-income families to help them pay for rent. Tax credit housing is housing that is privately owned but the government provides tax breaks to the developers to make it more affordable for low-income families.

Each type of affordable housing has its own benefits and drawbacks, but all of them provide a much needed service to low-income families who would otherwise be unable to afford a place to live.
