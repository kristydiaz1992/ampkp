The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, but not all of them may be right for you. Here are some of the most common types of affordable housing:

Public housing is housing that is owned and managed by the government. It is usually reserved for low-income families and individuals.

Section 8 housing is a government-funded program that helps low-income families pay for rent. Families who qualify for Section 8 housing assistance receive a voucher that they can use to help pay for rent at a qualified apartment or house.

Low-income housing tax credit (LIHTC) property is a type of affordable housing that is privately owned and operated. LIHTC properties offer reduced rents to qualifying families and individuals. In order to qualify for reduced rent at a LIHTC property, you must meet the income requirements set by the government.

Project-based Section 8 housing is a type of affordable housing that is owned and operated by private landlords. Like other forms of Section 8 housing, families who qualify for project-based Section 8 assistance receive a voucher that they can use to help pay for rent at a qualified apartment or house.

There are many other types of affordable housing, such as co-ops, supportive housing, and transitional housing. If you are looking for affordable housing, it is important to do your research to find the type of housing that is right for you and your family.
