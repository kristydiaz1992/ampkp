What is affordable housing and why is it important?


Affordable housing is housing that is deemed affordable to those with a median household income as determined by country, region, or city. In the United States, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) sets fair market rent (FMR) limits for housing, which is used as a benchmark for affordability. HUD determines FMR for each metropolitan area, and any housing that costs less than FMR is considered affordable.
