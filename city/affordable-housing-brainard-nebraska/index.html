The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, but most can be classified into three broad categories: public housing, subsidized housing, and income-restricted housing.

Public housing is owned and operated by the government, and is typically reserved for low-income households. Subsidized housing is privately owned but the government provides financial assistance to make it more affordable. Income-restricted housing is privately owned but there are restrictions on who can live there based on their income.

The federal government provided funding for the construction of public housing projects which were intended to provide safe and affordable homes for low-income families. Unfortunately, these projects were often plagued by poor design and management, and they quickly became associated with crime and poverty.

In the 1970s, the federal government began to shift its focus from public housing to providing subsidies for private developers to build affordable housing. This program, known as Section 8, was designed to incentivize developers to build housing that would be affordable for low- and moderate-income families. However, this program has been criticized for not doing enough to actually create affordable housing, as the subsidies are often used to offset the costs of luxury developments rather than to create truly affordable homes.
