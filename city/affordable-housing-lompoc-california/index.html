The difference between affordable housing and public housing


There are a few key differences between affordable housing and public housing. First, affordable housing is typically income-based, while public housing is needs-based. This means that affordable housing is available to those who meet a certain income threshold, while public housing is available to those who meet a certain need (such as being homeless or having a disability).

Second, affordable housing is typically privately owned, while public housing is owned by the government. This means that the government has more control over public housing, and can set stricter rules for who is eligible for living there.

Third, affordable housing is typically less expensive than public housing. This is because public housing is subsidized by the government, while affordable housing is not.

Overall, affordable housing and public housing are both great options for those who need assistance with housing costs. However, it’s important to understand the key differences between the two before making a decision.

What affordable housing options are available in Lompoc?



There are a variety of affordable housing options available in Lompoc, including public housing, Section 8 housing, and Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) properties. Public housing is managed by the Lompoc Housing Authority and provides safe, decent, and affordable housing to low- and moderate-income households. Section 8 housing is a federally-funded program that provides financial assistance to eligible families to help them afford safe, decent, and sanitary housing. LIHTC properties are privately-owned developments that provide affordable housing to low- and moderate-income households through the use of tax credits.
