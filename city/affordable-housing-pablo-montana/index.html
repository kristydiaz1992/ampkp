What You Need to Know About Affordable Housing





There is a lot of talk these days about affordable housing, but what does that actually mean? Affordable housing is housing that is considered affordable to households who are earning a certain income. In the United States, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) sets the income limits for households that qualify for affordable housing programs.

There are a number of different affordable housing programs available, each with its own income limits and requirements. Some of the most common programs include public housing, Section 8 housing vouchers, and low-income housing tax credits.

Public housing is housing that is owned and operated by government agencies. Public housing units are typically available to households that earn below a certain income limit. In order to qualify for public housing, households must also meet other criteria such as being U.S. citizens or legal residents, having a good rental history, and not having any outstanding evictions.

Section 8 housing vouchers are another type of affordable housing program. This program provides financial assistance to households that qualify, so that they can afford to rent privately owned homes or apartments. To qualify for a Section 8 voucher, households must again meet certain income limits and other requirements.

Low-income housing tax credits (LIHTC) are another way that affordable housing is made available. The LIHTC program provides tax credits to developers and investors who build or rehabilitate housing that is reserved for households that earn below a certain income limit. In order to qualify for the LIHTC, developments must also meet other requirements such as being located in designated “qualified census tracts” or “opportunity zones.”

If you are interested in finding out more about affordable housing programs, or if you think you may qualify for one of these programs, you should contact your local HUD office.
