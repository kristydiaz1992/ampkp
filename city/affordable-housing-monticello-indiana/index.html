Ways to make affordable housing more available in Monticello


There is no one silver bullet when it comes to increasing the availability of affordable housing. But there are a number of ways that cities and towns can make a difference. Here are a few ideas:

1. Inclusionary zoning: Adopt policies that require new developments to include a certain percentage of affordable units.

2. Density bonuses: Offer developers bonuses for increasing the density of their projects, which can help offset the costs of including affordable units.

3. Community land trusts: Create trusts that purchase and manage land for affordable housing developments.

4. Housing vouchers: Expand voucher programs to help low-income households afford rent.

5. Rental assistance: Provide financial assistance to families struggling to pay rent.

6. Housing production trust fund: Use public funds to finance the construction and preservation of affordable housing.

7. Impact fees: Charge developers fees that are used to fund affordable housing initiatives.

8. Housing tax credits: Provide tax incentives to encourage the development of affordable housing.

9. Zoning reform: Update zoning laws and regulations to allow for more density and mixed-use development, which can help bring down the costs of new housing.

10. Deregulation: Remove unnecessary regulations that increase the cost of building new housing.
