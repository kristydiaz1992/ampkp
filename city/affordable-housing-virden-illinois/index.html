The difference between affordable housing and public housing in Virden, Illinois



There are a few key differences between affordable housing and public housing in Virden, Illinois. For one, affordable housing is typically income-based, meaning that households must meet a certain income threshold in order to qualify. Public housing, on the other hand, is typically need-based, meaning that households must meet a certain need (such as being homeless, or having a disability) in order to qualify.

Another difference is that affordable housing is usually privately owned, while public housing is owned by the government. This means that the standards for maintenance and quality can vary widely between the two types of housing.

Finally, public housing typically has much more stringent eligibility requirements than affordable housing. For example, public housing may require that applicants have a certain criminal background or credit score. Affordable housing may have income requirements, but typically does not have the same kind of strict eligibility criteria.
