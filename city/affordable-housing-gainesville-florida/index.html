15 cheap and easy ways to make your rental unit more affordable


There's no need to spend a lot of money to make your rental unit more affordable. Here are 15 cheap and easy ways to do it:

1. Get energy-efficient appliances: ENERGY STAR® certified appliances use less energy, which can save you money on your utility bills.

2. Install a programmable thermostat: A programmable thermostat can help you save money by automatically setting the temperature back when you’re not home.

3. Use LED light bulbs: LED light bulbs use less energy than traditional incandescent light bulbs, and can last up to 25 times longer.

4. Use a power strip: Plugging all your electronics into a power strip that you can turn off when you’re not using them can help save energy and money.

5. Unplug electronics when you’re not using them: Even if an electronic is turned off, it can still use energy if it’s plugged in. So be sure to unplug electronics when you’re not using them.

6. Seal air leaks: Drafty windows and doors can let heat out in the winter and cool air out in the summer, costing you money. Sealing air leaks with weatherstripping or caulk can help reduce your energy bills.

7. Use a space heater: If you have a room that’s not used often, using a space heater to heat it instead of your whole home can save you money.

8. Add insulation: Adding insulation to your home can help keep heat in during the winter and cool air in during the summer.

9. Get a window air conditioner: Window air conditioners are a more affordable option than central air conditioning, and can still keep your home cool during the summer.

10. Use a ceiling fan: Ceiling fans can help circulate air in your home, and can make you feel cooler without having to lower the temperature.

11. Dress for the weather: Wearing layers of clothing or light-colored clothing can help you stay comfortable without having to raise the temperature.

12. Use window treatments: Closing curtains or blinds during the hottest part of the day can help keep your home cooler.

13. Use a slow cooker: Slow cookers use less energy than ovens, and can help you save money on your utility bills.

14. Hang clothes to dry: Using a clothesline or drying rack to air-dry your clothes instead of using a clothes dryer can save you money.

15. Use a fan: Fans use less energy than air conditioners, and can help circulate air in your home.

How to decorate your rental unit on a budget



If you're like most renters, you probably don't want to spend a lot of money on decorating your rental unit. After all, you're not going to be living there for very long and you don't want to put too much money into something that you're not going to get to keep. However, there are ways to decorate your rental unit on a budget. Here are a few tips:

-Paint is one of the cheapest ways to completely change the look of a room. If you're allowed to paint, pick a neutral color that will go with anything and won't be too difficult to paint over when you move out.

-Hang things up on your walls. Posters, pictures, and even tapestries can add a lot of personality to a room without costing very much. Just be sure to use damage-free hangers so you don't have to worry about patching up holes when you move out.

-Get creative with your furniture. If you can't afford to buy new furniture, try shopping at garage sales or thrift stores. You can usually find great pieces for a fraction of the price. And if you're really crafty, you can even try refinishing some of the furniture yourself.

-Use area rugs to define different spaces. Area rugs can be a great way to add color and pattern to a room without breaking the bank. Plus, they can be easily rolled up and taken with you when you move.

-Accessorize. Throw pillows, blankets, and even small pieces of art can make a big difference in the way a room looks. And the best part is, they can all be packed up and taken with you when you move.

By following these tips, you can decorate your rental unit on a budget and still make it feel like home.
