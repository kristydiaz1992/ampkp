Affordable housing is housing that is available at a price below the market rate. It is typically provided by government or nonprofit organizations. The goal of affordable housing is to make sure that everyone has a place to live that is safe, clean, and affordable.

Income Families

There are a number of government programs that provide assistance to low-income families, such as the Housing Choice Voucher program (formerly known as Section 8). However, these programs are often oversubscribed and have lengthy waiting lists.

One way to help low-income families afford housing is to provide subsidies or tax breaks. The Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) is a federal tax credit that incentivizes the development of affordable housing. States and localities often have their own programs as well.

Another way to increase the availability of affordable housing is to increase density. This can be done through zoning changes or by building accessory dwelling units (ADUs), also known as granny flats.

Accessory dwelling units are small, self-contained living units that are attached to or located on the same property as a single-family home. They can be used as rental units, in-law units, or even as a home office.

By increasing the density of housing, we can help to make it more affordable. This is because the cost of land and infrastructure is spread over a larger number of units, making each unit less expensive.

Accessory dwelling units are a particularly attractive option because they can be built relatively cheaply and quickly. They can also be built without displacing any existing residents.

If you’re interested in increasing the availability of affordable housing in your community, there are a number of things you can do. You can advocate for changes to zoning laws, work with your local government to create incentives for developers, or even build an accessory dwelling unit yourself.
