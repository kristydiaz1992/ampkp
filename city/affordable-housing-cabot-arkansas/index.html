The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, each with their own pros and cons. Here are a few of the most popular types of affordable housing:

1. Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) Properties
LIHTC properties are required to set aside a certain number of units for low-income residents, usually those making 60% or less of the area median income (AMI). LIHTC properties often have income and rent restrictions, as well as other rules designed to keep units affordable.

2. Project-Based Section 8 Properties
Project-based Section 8 properties are privately-owned rental properties that receive federal subsidies to help make units affordable for low-income residents. Like LIHTC properties, Project-based Section 8 properties often have income and rent restrictions.

3. Public Housing
Public housing is owned and operated by government entities, and is typically reserved for low-income residents. Public housing units may have income and rent restrictions, as well as other rules designed to keep units affordable.

4. Housing Choice (Section 8) Vouchers
Housing Choice vouchers are portable subsidies that can be used to help low-income renters afford units in the private market. Voucher holders can choose their own housing, as long as the unit meets program requirements and the landlord agrees to accept the voucher.

5. HUD-Insured and HUD-Assisted Properties
HUD-insured and HUD-assisted properties are privately-owned rental properties that receive federal subsidies to help make units affordable for low-income residents. Income and rent restrictions may apply.

Each type of affordable housing has its own advantages and disadvantages, so it's important to do your research to see which option is best for your needs.
