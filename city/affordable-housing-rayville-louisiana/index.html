The History of Affordable Housing

Early attempts at providing affordable housing were often ad hoc and piecemeal, and it wasn’t until the early 20th century that a more systematic approach began to take shape. The first major federal effort to address the issue was the Housing Act of 1937, which created the United States Housing Authority (USHA) and provided funding for the construction of public housing projects.

The USHA was initially quite successful, but by the 1950s, public housing had become increasingly associated with poverty and crime. The Different Ways Affordable Housing Can Be Implemented


Different ways that affordable housing can be implemented are by offering rent-controlled housing, by providing housing vouchers, or by building government-subsidized housing.

Rent-controlled housing is when the government sets a limit on how much landlords can charge for rent. This makes it affordable for tenants, as they know their rent will not increase beyond a certain amount.

Housing vouchers are another way to make housing affordable. Vouchers are given to low-income families which help to pay for rent. This subsidy makes it possible for families to live in housing that they may not otherwise be able to afford.

Government-subsidized housing is when the government provides financial assistance to developers in order to build housing that is affordable for low- and moderate-income families. This housing is usually built with the intention of it being affordable in the long-term.
