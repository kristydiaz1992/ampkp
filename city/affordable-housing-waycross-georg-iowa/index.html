These properties are made available to low-income earners through the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit program. The credits are given to the developers, who then pass on the savings to the tenants in the form of lower rents.

2. Public Housing

Public housing is owned and managed by government agencies. It is typically made available to low-income earners, although some properties may be open to all.

3. Section 8 Housing

Section 8 housing is a government-subsidized housing program. Tenants pay a portion of their income towards rent, and the government pays the remainder.

4. Rent-Controlled Apartments

Rent-controlled apartments are those in which the government has placed a limit on the amount of rent that can be charged. This is done in order to make housing more affordable for low-income earners.

5. Housing Cooperatives

Housing cooperatives are housing developments that are owned and managed by a cooperative of tenants. They typically have lower rents than comparable properties, as the tenants share in the costs of maintenance and upkeep.

6. Mobile Home Parks

Mobile home parks are communities of mobile homes that are typically owned by the residents. These can be a more affordable option than traditional housing, as the costs of land and infrastructure are shared among the residents.

7. supportive housing

Supportive housing is a type of housing that is specifically designed for those with mental health issues, addiction problems, or other disabilities. These properties typically have on-site support staff and services to help residents maintain their tenancy.

8. Housing Choice Vouchers

Housing choice vouchers are vouchers that can be used to rent properties from participating landlords. The voucher program is designed to help low-income earners find affordable housing.
