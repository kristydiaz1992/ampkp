What Are the Different Types of Affordable Housing?


There are many different types of affordable housing, but they can broadly be classified into two categories: government-sponsored housing and privately-sponsored housing.

Government-sponsored housing includes public housing, which is housing that is owned and managed by local governments. It is typically offered to low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities. Another type of government-sponsored housing is Section 8 housing, which is a voucher program that helps low-income families cover the cost of rent.

Privately-sponsored housing includes subsidized housing, which is housing that is subsidized by the government but owned and operated by private companies. Low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities typically qualify for this type of housing. Another type of privately-sponsored housing is affordable housing, which is housing that is not subsidized by the government but is still affordable for low- and moderate-income families.
