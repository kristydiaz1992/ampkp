The different types of affordable housing available in Santa Maria


There are many different types of affordable housing available in Santa Maria. Some of the most common types of affordable housing include:

Public Housing: Public housing is housing that is owned and operated by the government. Public housing is available to low- and moderate-income families, as well as the elderly and disabled.

Section 8 Housing: Section 8 housing is a federally-funded program that provides housing assistance to low- and moderate-income families. Families who participate in the Section 8 program pay a portion of their rent, with the government paying the remainder.

Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) Properties: LIHTC properties are privately-owned, affordable housing developments that receive tax credits from the government in exchange for providing affordable housing. LIHTC properties are available to low- and moderate-income families.

Project-Based Section 8 Housing: Project-based Section 8 housing is a type of affordable housing in which the rent is based on the income of the tenant. Project-based Section 8 housing is available to low- and moderate-income families.

Housing Choice Vouchers: Housing choice vouchers are a type of housing assistance that allows families to choose their own housing. Families who participate in the voucher program pay a portion of their rent, with the government paying the remainder.
