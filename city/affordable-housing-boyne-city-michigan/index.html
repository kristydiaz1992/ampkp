10 Myths About Affordable Housing Debunked


Are you looking for an affordable place to live, but feel like you can't find anything that's within your budget? You're not alone. A lot of people are under the impression that affordable housing is impossible to find, or that it only exists in low-quality, dangerous neighborhoods.

However, these beliefs are nothing more than myths. Here are 10 myths about affordable housing debunked:

1. There's no such thing as affordable housing.

This is simply not true. There are plenty of affordable housing options out there, you just need to know where to look.

2. Affordable housing is only available in low-income neighborhoods.

Again, this is not the case. While there are some affordable housing developments in low-income neighborhoods, there are also plenty in middle-class and even upper-class neighborhoods.

3. Affordable housing is only for low-income people.

Not true! Anyone can apply for affordable housing, regardless of their income.

4. Affordable housing is only for people with perfect credit.

Nope! While having good credit will certainly help your chances of getting approved for affordable housing, it's not a requirement.

5. Affordable housing is only for single people.

Families can also qualify for affordable housing. In fact, many affordable housing developments are specifically designed for families.

6. Affordable housing is only for people who are employed.

Again, not true. While having a job will obviously increase your chances of getting approved for affordable housing, there are many programs that specifically cater to people who are unemployed or underemployed.

7. Affordable housing is only for US citizens.

This is false. Anyone, regardless of their citizenship status, can apply for affordable housing.

8. Affordable housing is only for people who are eligible for government assistance.

Not true! Anyone can apply for affordable housing, regardless of their eligibility for government assistance.

9. Affordable housing is only for people who are willing to live in unsafe or rundown neighborhoods.

This is definitely not the case. While there are some affordable housing developments in less-than-desirable neighborhoods, there are also many in safe, clean, and well-maintained neighborhoods.

10. Affordable housing is too good to be true.

On the contrary, affordable housing is very real, and it can be an excellent option for people who are looking for a place to live that fits within their budget.
