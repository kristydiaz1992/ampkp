The rising cost of housing in Tewksbury and what can be done to make it more affordable


Housing costs in Tewksbury have been rising steadily for the past few years, and there is no end in sight. The average rent for a one bedroom apartment is now over $1,200, and the average price of a home is close to $350,000.

There are a number of factors driving up housing costs in Tewksbury, including a strong economy and a lack of available land. However, the biggest factor is the rising cost of construction. The price of lumber, concrete, and other building materials has skyrocketed in recent years, and this is being passed on to renters and buyers.

There is no easy solution to the problem of rising housing costs, but there are a few things that could be done to make it more affordable.

One option is to build more affordable housing developments, such as low-income apartments or condos. However, this is often easier said than done, as developers need to find ways to offset the higher costs of construction.

Another option is to provide incentives for developers to build more affordable housing. This could include offering tax breaks or fast-tracking approvals for developments that include a certain percentage of affordable units.

The town could also invest in affordable housing initiatives, such as creating a fund to help low-income residents with their rent or down payment.

Ultimately, the solution to the problem of rising housing costs will require a collaborative effort from developers, homeowners, renters, and the town government. By working together, we can make Tewksbury a more affordable place to live for everyone.
