What is affordable housing and why is it important?


Affordable housing is housing that is considered affordable to those with a median household income as determined by country, region, or locality. In the United States, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) sets fair market rents (FMRs) for housing units to ensure that housing is affordable for those who live in it. The FMR is calculated as the 40th percentile of rents for all housing units of a given size in a given area.
