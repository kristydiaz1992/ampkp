The Different Programs Offered for Affordable Housing


There are many affordable housing programs available to help low- and moderate-income families find a place to call home. Some of these programs are provided by the federal government, while others are offered by state or local governments.

The most well-known federal affordable housing program is the Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher program. This program provides voucher assistance to low-income families, which can be used to help pay for rent in privately-owned homes or apartments.

Another federal program is the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC). This program provides tax credits to developers and investors who build or rehabilitate housing that is affordable for low- and moderate-income families.

The Housing Choice Voucher program is administered by state and local housing agencies. These agencies work with participating landlords to provide voucher assistance to eligible families.

The LIHTC program is administered by the Internal Revenue Service. Developers and investors who participate in the program receive tax credits that can be used to offset the cost of building or rehabilitating affordable housing.

There are many other affordable housing programs available at the state and local level. These programs vary by location, but they may provide assistance with down payment and closing costs, rental assistance, or homeownership assistance.

If you are interested in finding an affordable housing program in your area, contact your local housing agency or the Internal Revenue Service.
