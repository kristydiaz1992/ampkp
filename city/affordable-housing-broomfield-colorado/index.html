gentrification and affordable housing


Gentrification is the process by which a neighborhood changes from being primarily populated by low-income residents to being primarily populated by middle- or upper-income residents. This often happens when wealthier people move into a neighborhood, causing the cost of living to increase and pushing out the lower-income residents who can no longer afford to live there.

Affordable housing is housing that is affordable to low- and moderate-income households. It is often subsidized in some way, either through government funding or through private developers who agree to set aside a certain number of units for low-income residents.

The two issues are often intertwined, as gentrification can lead to the loss of affordable housing. When wealthier people move into a neighborhood and drive up the cost of living, lower-income residents who can no longer afford to live there may be forced to move to a less expensive neighborhood. This can lead to the displacement of long-time residents and the loss of affordable housing units in the neighborhood.
