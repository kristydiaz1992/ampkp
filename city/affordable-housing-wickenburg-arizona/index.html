The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, each with their own benefits and drawbacks. Here are some of the most common types of affordable housing:

Public Housing: Public housing is housing that is owned and operated by a government entity, usually at the local level. Public housing is typically reserved for low-income households, although there may be some income restrictions in place. The biggest benefit of public housing is that it is usually very affordable, as the government subsidizes a portion of the rent. However, public housing can also be quite crowded and may not be well-maintained.

Section 8 Housing: Section 8 housing is a program administered by the US Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). Under this program, low-income households receive a voucher that can be used to help pay rent at a privately-owned apartment or house. The biggest benefit of Section 8 housing is that it gives voucher holders more flexibility in where they live, as they are not limited to public housing developments. However, voucher holders may still have difficulty finding an affordable place to live, as landlords often charge more than the voucher covers.

Low-Income Housing Tax Credits: The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) program is a federal program that provides tax credits to developers who build or rehabilitate housing that is reserved for low-income households. The LIHTC program is administered by state and local governments, and the amount of the tax credit depends on the area where the housing is located. The biggest benefit of the LIHTC program is that it incentivizes the construction of new affordable housing units. However, the tax credits are only available for a limited time, so developers may not continue to operate the housing units as affordable housing after the tax credits expire.

Community Land Trusts: Community land trusts are nonprofit organizations that develop and manage housing that is affordable for low- and moderate-income households. Community land trusts typically acquire land and then lease it to households or developers, with the stipulation that the housing built on the land must be affordable. The biggest benefit of community land trusts is that they allow for the permanent preservation of affordable housing. However, community land trusts can be difficult to establish and may not have the resources to manage a large amount of housing.
