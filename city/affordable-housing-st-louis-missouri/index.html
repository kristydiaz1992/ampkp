The need for affordable housing in St. Louis


The St. Louis region is in the midst of an affordable housing crisis. For generations, the city has been a place of opportunity for working families, but that is no longer the case. The rise in housing costs, coupled with stagnant wages, has made it increasingly difficult for families to find safe, decent, and affordable housing. According to a recent study, more than half of all renters in the region are “cost-burdened,” meaning they spend more than 30 percent of their income on housing. For those who are “severely cost-burdened,” the figure is even higher, at more than 50 percent.

The lack of affordable housing has far-reaching consequences. It forces families to make difficult choices between paying the rent and other essentials like food and healthcare. It can lead to overcrowding and substandard living conditions. It can cause children to change schools frequently, disrupting their education. And it can trap families in a cycle of poverty that is difficult to escape.

The city of St. Louis has taken some steps to address the affordable housing crisis, but more needs to be done. The city should increase its investment in affordable housing, create new incentives for developers to build more affordable units, and streamline the permitting process to make it easier to build housing that is both safe and affordable.

There is no single solution to the affordable housing crisis, but it is clear that the status quo is not working. The city of St. Louis must take bold action to ensure that its residents have access to safe, decent, and affordable housing.
