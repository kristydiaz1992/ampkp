Glossary of Affordable Housing Terms


The term "affordable housing" is often used to describe housing that is income-based. However, there are many other terms used to describe different types of affordable housing. Here is a glossary of some common affordable housing terms:

*Affordable Housing: Housing that is available and affordable for low- and moderate-income households.

*Bond-Financed Housing: Housing that is financed through the sale of tax-exempt bonds.

*Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs): Nonprofit organizations that provide financing for affordable housing and community development projects.

*Credit Enhancement: A financial tool that helps make financing for affordable housing projects more accessible. Credit enhancement can take the form of a loan guarantee, insurance, or a line of credit.

*Eligible Activities: Activities that are eligible for funding under the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit program. Eligible activities include the acquisition, rehabilitation, and new construction of affordable housing.

*Housing Choice Vouchers: A type of assistance that helps low-income households rent safe, decent, and affordable housing in the private market.

*Income-Based Housing: Housing that is affordable for households at or below a certain income level.

*Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC): A federal tax credit that incentivizes the development of affordable housing.

*Project-Based Rental Assistance: A type of assistance that helps make rent affordable for low-income households. Project-based rental assistance is provided through a contract between the housing owner and a government agency.

*Public Housing: Housing that is owned and operated by a government agency. Public housing is typically reserved for low-income households.

*Section 8: A type of assistance that helps low-income households rent safe, decent, and affordable housing in the private market. Section 8 is a program administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development.

*Supportive Housing: Housing that is combined with supportive services to help residents with special needs, such as the elderly, disabled, or homeless.
