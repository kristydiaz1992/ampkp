1. Low-Income Housing Tax Credit Program

The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) program is a federal tax credit program that helps developers build and finance affordable housing developments for low- and moderate-income families. The tax credit is equal to a percentage of the development’s cost, and it can be used to offset the developer’s federal income tax liability.

2. HUD Multifamily Housing Programs

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) offers a variety of multifamily housing programs that provide financing for the development, rehabilitation, and acquisition of affordable housing developments. These programs can be used to finance both market-rate and affordable housing developments.

3. Community Development Block Grants

Community Development Block Grants (CDBG) are federal grants that are awarded to states and local governments to be used for community development and affordable housing initiatives. CDBG funds can be used to finance a variety of activities, including the construction of new affordable housing developments, the rehabilitation of existing affordable housing developments, and the acquisition of land for future affordable housing developments.

4. Tax-Exempt Bonds

Tax-exempt bonds are a type of financing that can be used to finance the construction of new affordable housing developments. Tax-exempt bonds are issued by state and local governments and are exempt from federal, state, and local taxes. The interest on these bonds is typically lower than the interest on other types of financing, which makes them an attractive option for developers.
