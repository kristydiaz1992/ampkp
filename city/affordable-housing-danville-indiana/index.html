Another factor that contributes to the high cost of housing is the regulatory environment. Cities have a number of regulations in place that make it difficult and expensive to build new housing. For example, many cities have minimum parking requirements, which mandate that new developments must include a certain number of parking spots. These requirements add to the cost of building new housing, and make it more difficult to build affordable housing.

The high cost of housing is also due in part to the way that our tax system is structured. The federal government provides a number of tax breaks for homeowners, but not for renters. This encourages people to buy homes, which drives up the price of housing.

There are a number of political obstacles to creating more affordable housing. These obstacles include the lack of available land, the regulatory environment, and the tax system. Overcoming these obstacles will require a concerted effort from elected officials, developers, and the general public.
