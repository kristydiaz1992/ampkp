The cost of living in Chandler, Arizona


If you're thinking of moving to Chandler, Arizona, you may be wondering what the cost of living is like. Here's a breakdown of some of the most important expenses you'll face:

Housing: The median price for a home in Chandler is $262,000, which is relatively affordable compared to other parts of the country. Renting is also an option, with the average price for a two-bedroom apartment being $1,200 per month.

Utilities: Chandler residents can expect to pay an average of $140 per month for electricity, gas, and water.

Transportation: If you plan on owning a car in Chandler, you'll need to budget for gas, insurance, and maintenance costs. Public transportation is also an option, with the city's Valley Metro system offering bus and light rail service. A monthly pass costs $60.

Food: Chandler has a variety of grocery stores, including Safeway, Fry's, and Bashas'. The average cost of a week's worth of groceries for a family of four is $200.

Health Care: There are a number of hospitals and clinics in Chandler, including Banner Health and Dignity Health. The average cost of a doctor's visit is $100.

Education: Chandler is home to several colleges and universities, including Chandler-Gilbert Community College and Arizona State University. Tuition and fees for a year of college can range from $2,000 to $10,000, depending on the school and program.

Overall, the cost of living in Chandler is relatively affordable. With a variety of housing options and reasonable prices for groceries, transportation, and health care, you can easily budget for a comfortable life in this Arizona city.
