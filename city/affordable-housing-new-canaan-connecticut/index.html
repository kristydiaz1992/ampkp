How to qualify for affordable housing in New Canaan


If you are looking for affordable housing in New Canaan, there are a few things you need to know in order to qualify. First, you must be a U.S. citizen or have a green card. Second, you must have a total household income that is below 80% of the area median income. Lastly, you must not have any assets that exceed the limit set by the program. If you meet all of these criteria, you should contact the New Canaan Housing Authority to begin the application process.

The different types of affordable housing in New Canaan


There are a few different types of affordable housing in New Canaan. The first type is public housing, which is owned and operated by the government. Public housing units are usually reserved for low-income families and individuals, and the rent is subsidized by the government. Another type of affordable housing is Section 8 housing, which is also subsidized by the government. Section 8 housing is available to low-income families and individuals, as well as to people with disabilities. Lastly, there is affordable housing that is privately owned and operated. This type of housing is not subsidized by the government, but the rent is still considered to be affordable for low-income families and individuals.
