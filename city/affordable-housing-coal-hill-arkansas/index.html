What Are the Barriers to Affordable Housing?



There are many barriers to affordable housing, but some of the most common are:

1. Lack of available land: This is often the most significant barrier to affordable housing development. Land is a finite resource, and when demand is high, prices can skyrocket. This can make it difficult or impossible for developers to find land that is both affordable and suitable for housing.

2. Zoning and land-use regulations: Zoning and land-use regulations can also be a significant barrier to affordable housing development. These regulations can dictate what types of development are allowed on a piece of land, and can make it difficult or impossible to develop housing that is affordable.

3. NIMBYism: NIMBYism, or Not In My Backyard syndrome, is a major barrier to affordable housing development. This is the tendency of people to oppose any development that they feel will negatively impact their community, even if that development would provide much-needed housing.

4. Lack of funding: Affordable housing development can be expensive, and often requires public or philanthropic funding to be viable. This funding can be difficult to secure, and can be a major barrier to affordable housing development.

5. Gentrification: Gentrification is a major barrier to affordable housing. This is the process by which wealthier people move into an area and drive up property values, making it difficult or impossible for lower-income residents to afford to stay.
