The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, each with their own benefits and drawbacks. The most common types of affordable housing are public housing, subsidized housing, and co-operative housing.

Public housing is housing that is owned and managed by the government. Public housing is usually reserved for low-income families and individuals, although there are some public housing developments that are available to middle-income earners as well. Public housing developments are usually located in urban areas, and they often suffer from poor maintenance and high crime rates.

Subsidized housing is housing that is privately owned but subsidized by the government. Like public housing, subsidized housing is usually reserved for low-income families and individuals. However, subsidized housing is often of better quality than public housing, and it is usually located in safer neighborhoods.

Co-operative housing is housing that is owned and managed by a group of people, rather than by the government or a private landlord. Co-operative housing developments are usually located in urban areas, and they often have amenities such as swimming pools, laundry facilities, and community centers. Co-operative housing developments are usually open to people of all income levels.

The History of Affordable Housing


The first affordable housing developments in the United States were built in the 1930s as part of the New Deal. The PWA built more than 2,000 projects across the country, including the first public housing development in New York City.

The second program, the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), provided mortgage insurance to private developers building affordable housing. The FHA insured more than 1 million mortgages between 1934 and 1962, and helped to finance the construction of nearly 6 million homes.

The third program, the Housing and Home Finance Agency (HHFA), was created in 1954 to consolidate the federal government’s housing programs. The HHFA built or financed more than 600,000 units of affordable housing.

The fourth and final program, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), was established in 1965. HUD’s mission is to “build strong, sustainable, inclusive communities and quality affordable homes for all.” HUD has helped to finance the construction of more than 3 million units of affordable housing.
