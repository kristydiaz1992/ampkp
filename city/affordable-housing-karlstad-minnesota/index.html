5 Myths About Affordable Housing – Debunked!



There's a lot of misinformation out there about affordable housing. Here are five of the most common myths, debunked!

1. Affordable housing is only for low-income people.

Wrong! Affordable housing is for anyone who cannot afford to pay market rent or prices for housing. This includes people with low incomes, but also middle-income earners who are struggling to make ends meet.

2. Affordable housing is only for people on welfare.

Again, wrong! While affordable housing does help people on low incomes, it is also for people who are working but still can't afford market rent or prices.

3. Affordable housing is only for people who have never owned a home before.

Nope! Affordable housing is for anyone who cannot afford to pay market rent or prices for housing, regardless of whether they have owned a home before.

4. Affordable housing is only for people who live in cities.

Not true! While affordable housing is more common in cities, there are also programs for rural areas.

5. Affordable housing is only for people who are single/have no children.

False! Affordable housing is for anyone who cannot afford to pay market rent or prices for housing, regardless of their family situation.
