The civil rights movement of the 1960s brought attention to the fact that minorities were being discriminated against when it came to housing. The Fair Housing Act of 1968 made it illegal to discriminate against potential renters or buyers on the basis of race, religion, or national origin. This Act helped open up the housing market to more Americans, but there is still work to be done in terms of ensuring that everyone has access to affordable housing.
