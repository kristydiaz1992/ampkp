The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, and each has its own advantages and disadvantages. The most common types of affordable housing are public housing, voucher-based housing, and tax-credit housing.

Public housing is government-owned and -operated housing that is typically reserved for low-income families. Public housing is usually less expensive than other types of housing, but it can be more difficult to get into because there is often a waiting list.

Voucher-based housing is housing that is subsidized by the government, but it is not owned or operated by the government. Vouchers are given to low-income families, who can then use them to help pay for rent. The advantage of voucher-based housing is that it gives families more choice in where they live. The disadvantage is that it can be difficult to find landlords who accept vouchers.

Tax-credit housing is privately owned and operated, but it is subsidized by the government. Tax credits are given to the developers of these housing projects, which helps to make the housing more affordable. The advantage of tax-credit housing is that it is available in many different locations. The disadvantage is that it is often not as affordable as public housing or voucher-based housing.

This program provided subsidies for the construction of low-income housing and also helped to create the Federal Housing Administration, which provided loans for the construction of new homes.

After the Great Depression, the issue of affordable housing largely disappeared from the national conversation until the 1960s, when a growing number of Americans began to call for more government action on the issue.
