The difference between low income housing and affordable housing


There are a few key differences between low income housing and affordable housing. For one, low income housing is typically government-subsidized, meaning that the government covers a portion of the rent. This is often done in order to help low income families or individuals who may struggle to find affordable housing on their own. Affordable housing, on the other hand, is not government-subsidized and is instead priced based on the market. This means that it may still be out of reach for some low income families or individuals. Additionally, affordable housing is often designated for specific groups of people, such as seniors, low income families, or the disabled. Low income housing is not typically designated for a specific group and is available to anyone who meets the income requirements.
