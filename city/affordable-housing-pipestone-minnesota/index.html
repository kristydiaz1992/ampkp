5 Myths About Affordable Housing in Minnesota


There's a lot of talk these days about affordable housing and the need for more of it, especially in expensive urban areas. But what exactly is affordable housing? And what are the myths about it?

In Minnesota, affordable housing is defined as housing that costs no more than 30% of a household's gross income. This includes rent or mortgage payments, as well as utility bills.

There are a lot of myths out there about affordable housing, so let's dispel some of them:

1. Affordable housing is only for low-income families.

Wrong! While many low-income families do benefit from affordable housing, it's not just for them. Middle-income families can also struggle to find housing that doesn't cost a fortune.

2. Affordable housing is only for people who can't find a job.

Again, wrong! Affordable housing is for anyone who struggles to pay their housing costs. This includes people who are working but don't make enough to cover their housing expenses.

3. Affordable housing is only for people who are on government assistance.

Nope! Anyone who needs help paying for housing can benefit from affordable housing.

4. Affordable housing is only for people who live in poverty.

This one is also not true. Affordable housing is for anyone who struggles to pay their rent or mortgage.

5. Affordable housing is a handout.

This is perhaps the biggest myth of all. Affordable housing is not a handout. It's simply a way to make sure that everyone has a place to live that they can afford.
