The Different Types of Affordable Housing


There are many types of affordable housing, each with its own unique benefits. Here are some of the most popular types of affordable housing:

1. Low-Income Housing: Low-income housing is typically government-funded and reserved for households that earn below a certain income threshold. Low-income housing can be either rental or ownership housing.

2. Senior Housing: Senior housing is designed for older adults who may need assistance with daily activities. Senior housing can be either rental or ownership housing.

3. Section 8 Housing: Section 8 housing is a federally-funded program that provides housing vouchers to low-income households. Section 8 housing can be either rental or ownership housing.

4. Public Housing: Public housing is owned and operated by government agencies. Public housing is typically reserved for low-income households.

5. Housing Choice Vouchers: Housing choice vouchers are federally-funded vouchers that can be used to rent or purchase housing from any participating landlord. Housing choice vouchers can be used in any type of housing, including low-income housing, senior housing, and public housing.

6. Homeownership Assistance Programs: Homeownership assistance programs are designed to help low- and moderate-income households purchase homes. These programs may offer down payment assistance, low-interest loans, and other forms of financial assistance.

7. Rental Assistance Programs: Rental assistance programs provide financial assistance to low-income households who are renting homes. Rental assistance programs may offer rent subsidies, utility assistance, and other forms of financial assistance.

8. Community Land Trusts: Community land trusts are nonprofit organizations that develop and manage affordable housing. Community land trusts often own the land on which the affordable housing is built, making it easier to keep the housing affordable in the long term.

9. Housing Cooperatives: Housing cooperatives are housing developments that are owned and operated by their residents. Housing cooperatives typically have rules in place to keep the housing affordable for all residents.

10. Manufactured Housing Communities: Manufactured housing communities are housing developments that are made up of factory-built homes. Manufactured housing communities often have rules in place to keep the housing affordable for all residents.

What is Affordable Housing?


The term “affordable housing” is used to describe dwelling units whose occupants do not spend more than 30% of their household income on housing costs. Affordable housing includes both rentals and ownership housing.

There are a number of ways to make housing affordable for low- and moderate-income households, including:

-Rental subsidies
-Public housing
-Inclusionary zoning
-Tax incentives
-Mortgage assistance programs

Rental subsidies are government payments made to landlords on behalf of tenants. The payments are based on the difference between the rent charged and the amount the tenant can afford to pay.

Public housing is owned and operated by government agencies. It is typically reserved for low-income households.

Inclusionary zoning is a land-use planning tool that requires developers to set aside a certain percentage of units in new developments for low- and moderate-income households.

Tax incentives can be used to encourage developers to build affordable housing. For example, the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit provides developers with a federal tax credit for building affordable rental housing.

Mortgage assistance programs can help low- and moderate-income households afford homeownership. For example, the Federal Housing Administration’s (FHA) Homeownership Voucher Program provides subsidies to help families with children pay their mortgage.
