The Various Forms of Affordable Housing


There are many forms of affordable housing available to those in need. Some are government-sponsored, while others are privately owned and operated. Here are just a few of the most common types of affordable housing:

Public Housing: Public housing is owned and managed by government entities, such as local housing authorities. These homes are typically reserved for low-income families and individuals, as well as the elderly and disabled.

Section 8 Housing: The Section 8 program is a federally funded housing assistance program that provides vouchers to low-income families to help cover the cost of rent.

Low-Income Housing Tax Credits: The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) program is a government-sponsored program that provides tax credits to developers and investors who build or rehabilitate affordable housing units for low-income households.

Affordable Housing Trust Funds: Affordable housing trust funds are public or private funds that are used to finance the development or preservation of affordable housing.

These are just a few of the many types of affordable housing that are available. Each has its own eligibility requirements and benefits, so be sure to research the options that are available to you.
